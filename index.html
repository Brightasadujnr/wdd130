Ghana-Kumasi

Kumasi is a city and the capital of Ashanti Region of Ghana. Kumasi is a commercial, economic, and trading hub in Ghana, home to the biggest market in West Africa: the Kejetia Market.Kumasi derived from the Twi word Kumase, meaning 'under the kum tree' in which kum meant 'the tree' and ase means 'under'. The population of the city of Kumasi is 443,981 people while the population of the metropolitan area is 3,490,030 people. The city is home to numerous recreational parks and gardens which are open to the public, with the per capita green spaces around 4.7 square metres (51 sq ft).
